# Tone Guide

## Brand Voice
- **Professional but Conversational:** We are experts in portable toilets, but we speak to humans. No overly stiff corporate jargon.
- **Empathetic:** Planning an event is stressful. Acknowledge the pain points (guests complaining about toilets, bad smells, long lines) and offer our luxury solution.
- **Confident:** We know our toilets are the best (clean, luxurious, off-grid). Don't be afraid to highlight that.

## Style Rules
- **No Fluff:** Get straight to the point. Every sentence should add value. No "In today's fast-paced world..." introductions.
- **Formatting:** Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), bullet points, and bold text for skimmability.
- **E-E-A-T Injection:** Every blog MUST include at least one trust signal. Use a quote from a satisfied customer, mention our years of experience, or cite a real-world example of an event we serviced.
- **Unique Angle:** Never write "another version of what exists". Find a gap in the current SERP and fill it.
- **Competitor Mentions:** Be respectful. Acknowledge what they do well, but clearly define where our luxury offering is superior.
- **Call to Action:** Keep it soft, place it at the end (or naturally within the text). Example: "If you're planning an event and want to ensure your guests have a premium experience, contact us for a quote."
